Khan Bank Product Risk Rating Methodology

Each Khan Bank investment product is assigned a risk rating from 1 (Conservative) to 6 (Very Aggressive) using a transparent, multi-parameter weighted scoring model. The methodology is aligned with international best practices from MAS (Singapore), ESMA PRIIP, and leading private banks, adapted for the Mongolian market context.

⚖️ Scoring Formula

Rating = f(Weighted Score)

Raw Score =
Capital at Risk × 30% +
Market Volatility × 25% +
Credit Risk × 20% +
Liquidity Risk × 15% +
Product Complexity × 5% +
Investment Horizon × 5%

Final Rating = ceil(Raw) if decimal ≥ 0.15
floor(Raw) if decimal < 0.15
Leverage override: Final Rating = 6

The 0.15 threshold prevents minor parameter differences from causing arbitrary rating jumps. Leverage is an unconditional override — any leveraged product is automatically Rating 6 regardless of other parameters.

🔒 Override Rules

  • Leverage / Margin: Any product with borrowing or margin trading is automatically Rating 6, regardless of other parameter scores. No exceptions.
  • Unrated / Distressed Issuers: For bonds from unrated or CCC-rated issuers, minimum Credit Risk = 5. Final rating cannot be lower than 4.
  • Annual Review: All product ratings must be reviewed at minimum annually, or immediately upon material change in issuer credit quality, market conditions, or product structure.
  • New Products: Any product not listed in the approved library must be rated by the Product Risk Committee before client distribution.

📊 Parameter Level Definitions

1. Capital at Risk (30%)

1Fully protected — deposit guarantee or capital protection
2Capital protected with conditions (e.g. hold to maturity)
3Low risk of loss — <5% capital at risk in stress scenario
4Moderate — 5–25% potential capital loss
5Significant — 25–100% potential capital loss
6Full loss + beyond — leveraged, losses exceed principal

2. Market Volatility (25%)

1<1% annual std dev (deposits, T-bills)
21–3% (short-term government bonds)
33–8% (medium bonds, balanced)
48–18% (equity funds, HY bonds)
518–35% (individual equities, EM)
6>35% (derivatives, leveraged products)

3. Credit / Counterparty Risk (20%)

1Government-guaranteed / deposit-insured
2AAA–AA sovereign / quasi-sovereign
3A–BBB investment grade corporate
4BB below investment grade (high yield)
5B / CCC speculative grade
6Unrated / distressed / near default

4. Liquidity Risk (15%)

1Daily, T+0 to T+1 (deposits, money market)
2T+1 to T+3, daily dealing (open-end funds)
3T+3 to T+7, secondary market trading
4Monthly or quarterly redemption windows
5Annual or longer lock-up periods
6Fully illiquid — no redemption option

5. Product Complexity (5%)

1Simple and transparent (deposits, plain bonds)
2Straightforward (open-end vanilla funds)
3Moderate (structured deposits, optionality)
4Complex (multi-asset, hybrid instruments)
5Highly complex (exotic structures, path-dependent)
6Very complex (derivatives, CDOs, leveraged)

6. Investment Horizon (5%)

1Less than 3 months
23 months to 1 year
31 to 3 years
43 to 5 years
55 to 10 years
6More than 10 years / perpetual

🔗 Client–Product Matching Rule

Client risk rating (from profiling) must be ≥ product risk rating for a standard suitable recommendation.

ScenarioAction
Product rating ≤ Client rating✅ Fully suitable — proceed
Product rating = Client rating + 1⚠️ Client acknowledgment + RM note on file
Product rating = Client rating + 2🚫 Formal waiver + Senior RM / Compliance sign-off
Product rating > Client rating + 2🛑 Cannot proceed — re-profiling or PI assessment required
